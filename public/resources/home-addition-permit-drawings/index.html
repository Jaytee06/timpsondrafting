Home Addition Permit Drawings

What to gather and coordinate before drafting plans for a residential addition.

By Timpson Drafting & Design · Updated July 20, 2026

On this pageStart with the projectQuestions to resolveLimits and professional roles

Start with the actual project

A useful answer depends on the project location, existing conditions, intended work, available source files, and the authority that will review the drawings. Gather those facts before assuming a standard package, fee, or schedule applies.

For remote work, organized photos, labeled measurements, surveys, sketches, and existing plans reduce uncertainty. For additions, remodels, and as-built work, field verification may be the responsible next step.

Questions to resolve before drafting

  • Which city or county has jurisdiction?
  • What is being built or changed?
  • Which existing documents can be relied upon?
  • What sheets and supporting documents does the building department require?
  • Are structural, civil, energy, geotechnical, or other consultants required?
  • Who will answer design questions and approve revisions?

Permits, stamps, and limitations

Requirements vary. A drafter can document a defined residential scope, but drafting alone does not replace architecture or engineering when a licensed professional or stamp is required. Permit approval is controlled by the reviewing authority.
