# Branch: Missing Fields

## Node Type

Branch outcome.

## Meaning

The lead appears real and contactable, but the available project information is not sufficient for normal qualification.

## Typical Triggers

- vague description,
- missing project type,
- incomplete or unclear identity,
- unclear scope or timeline,
- contradictions between older stored lead data and the newest substantive reply.

## Local Objective

Request only the minimum missing information required to move the lead back toward qualification.

If the lead's service type is already known, prefer a service-specific question set over a generic catch-all request.

If the newest reply contradicts older stored data, ask the narrowest clarifying question needed to resolve the contradiction before escalating.

## Allowed Local Decisions

- typically keep or move the lead to `fresh` or `contacted`, depending on whether outreach has already occurred,
- send a concise clarification request,
- send a contradiction-resolving clarification instead of a generic `no_response` follow-up when the lead already replied with substantive details,
- continue clarification when the lead is responsive but not yet internally consistent,
- preserve all known attribution and contact context,
- move the lead toward `contacted` after outreach.

## Escalation Rules

- required fields are disputed across sibling workflows,
- the request is so vague that it may be out of scope entirely,
- the lead remains unresolved after normal clarification work and needs real human review,
- the CRM intake model lacks a place to store missing-info status.
