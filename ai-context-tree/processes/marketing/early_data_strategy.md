# Early Data Marketing Strategy

## Node Type

Strategy guide.

## Purpose

This file defines how the Marketing Agent should act when Google Ads has started receiving impressions and clicks but does not yet have enough conversion volume for confident CPA optimization.

## Current Evidence Pattern

In the observed Advisor run, `Campaign #1` showed:

- renewed traffic after a previous zero-activity period,
- low spend and low click volume,
- no recorded conversions,
- undefined CPA,
- national plan brokers and marketplaces in Auction Insights,
- early click interest around small-house, package/pricing, and room-addition terms.

This is not enough data for aggressive account restructuring.

## Strategic Principle

While conversion data is sparse, prioritize:

- tracking health,
- message clarity,
- small reversible landing page improvements,
- query intent documentation,
- weekly evidence collection.

Avoid:

- major budget increases,
- bidding strategy changes,
- broad campaign restructures,
- overfitting to one week of clicks,
- assuming click-heavy terms are high quality before conversion or CRM evidence exists.

## Recommended Near-Term Strategy

### 1. Verify Conversion Health First

Before optimizing toward CPA, confirm whether form submissions and offline conversion uploads are actually reaching Google Ads.

Checks:

- confirm the website fires the intended Google Ads conversion event after successful lead submission,
- confirm `gclid`, `gbraid`, and `wbraid` are preserved into the CRM where possible,
- confirm Data Manager API or offline upload logs receive valid click IDs from live traffic,
- compare CRM leads during the reporting window against Google Ads recorded conversions.

If Google Ads shows zero conversions but CRM has matching paid leads, treat tracking as the first implementation priority.

### 2. Position Against National Plan Brokers

Auction Insights showed national brokers and marketplaces. The response should emphasize Timpson Drafting's differentiation:

- local drafting expertise,
- permit-ready plans,
- custom residential work,
- homeowner and contractor collaboration,
- additions, remodels, garages, ADUs, and practical construction documents.

Do not try to win only by top-of-page rate while data is sparse. Improve message match first.

### 3. Add Specificity To Existing Landing Page Coverage

The site already includes:

- `Residential Drafting`,
- `Garage & Addition Plans`,
- `Remodel & Renovation Drawings`,
- `Permit-Ready Construction Documents`,
- `Custom Home Design Support`,
- `Basic Drafting Packages`,
- `Garages & Additions`,
- `Custom Residential Projects`.

The likely improvement is to make specific search intent more visible:

- small and efficient home plans,
- room additions such as master suites and mudrooms,
- package/pricing language that explains how quotes are scoped without promising fixed prices,
- remodel and addition examples that match homeowner search language.

### 4. Build A Weekly Query Watchlist

Track early interest terms separately from proven converters.

Initial watchlist from Advisor:

- `small house designs`,
- `house packages with prices`,
- `master bedroom addition plans`,
- `building a mudroom`,
- `modern 500 sq ft house`,
- `15x40 house plan`.

For each weekly probe, classify each term as:

- worth landing page copy,
- worth SEO content,
- worth ad asset testing,
- needs negative keyword review,
- too early to decide.

### 5. Use Low-Risk Implementation First

Recommended first implementation candidates:

- add a landing page subsection or service detail text for `Small & Efficient Home Plans`,
- add example text under additions for `master suites`, `mudrooms`, garages, ADUs, and remodels,
- adjust pricing copy to mention scoped drafting packages and custom quotes without promising fixed package prices,
- draft ad asset/headline variants around `Local Custom Drafting`, `Permit-Ready Remodel Plans`, and `Small Home Plans`.

Require approval before:

- adding new Google Ads asset groups,
- changing budgets,
- changing bidding strategy,
- accepting Google Ads terms,
- adding fixed prices or package promises,
- claiming service areas or permit outcomes not already verified.

## Suggested Strategy Statement

For the next two to four weekly probes, the Marketing Agent should treat `Campaign #1` as a learning campaign. The primary objective is to confirm tracking, collect enough click and conversion evidence, and tighten message match around early search-interest clusters. Landing page changes should clarify existing services rather than introduce unverified offers. Account changes should remain small and reversible until conversions or CRM-qualified lead feedback show which search themes are valuable.
